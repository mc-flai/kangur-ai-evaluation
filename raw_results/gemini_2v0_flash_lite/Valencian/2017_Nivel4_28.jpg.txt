Raonament:
1.  **Circumscribed Circle**: The diameter *D* of the circumscribed circle of a right-angled triangle is equal to the hypotenuse.  Using the Pythagorean theorem, the hypotenuse is √(a² + b²).
2.  **Inscribed Circle**: For a right-angled triangle, the diameter *d* of the inscribed circle is given by d = a + b - √(a² + b²).
3.  **Sum d + D**: Therefore, d + D = (a + b - √(a² + b²)) + √(a² + b²) = a + b.
