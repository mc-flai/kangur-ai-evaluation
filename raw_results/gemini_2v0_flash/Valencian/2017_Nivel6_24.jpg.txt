$Area(ABY) = \frac{1}{2} AB \cdot BY = 1+2 = 3$
$Area(ADX) = \frac{1}{2} AD \cdot AX = 1+x = 1+x$
$Area(CDY) = \frac{1}{2} CD \cdot CY = 3$
$Area(BCX) = \frac{1}{2} BC \cdot BX = 2$

Si $A(rectangle) = AB \cdot BC$.

$\frac{AX}{AB} = a$, $\frac{BY}{BC} = b$.
$AX = a AB$, $BY = b BC$.
$BX = (1-a)AB$, $CY = (1-b)BC$.

$A(ABY) = \frac{1}{2} AB \cdot BY = \frac{1}{2} AB \cdot b BC = 3$, $\frac{1}{2} AB \cdot BC b = 3$.
$A(ADX) = \frac{1}{2} AD \cdot AX = \frac{1}{2} BC \cdot a AB = 1+x$, $\frac{1}{2} AB \cdot BC a = 1+x$.
$A(CDY) = \frac{1}{2} CD \cdot CY = \frac{1}{2} AB (1-b)BC = 3$, $\frac{1}{2} AB \cdot BC (1-b) = 3$.
$A(BCX) = \frac{1}{2} BC \cdot BX = \frac{1}{2} BC (1-a)AB = 2$, $\frac{1}{2} AB \cdot BC (1-a) = 2$.

$\frac{1}{2} A b = 3$, $b = \frac{6}{A}$.
$\frac{1}{2} A a = 1+x$, $a = \frac{2(1+x)}{A}$.
