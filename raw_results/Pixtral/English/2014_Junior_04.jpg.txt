### Reasoning:
To determine which number is an equal distance from \(\frac{2}{3}\) and \(\frac{4}{5}\) on the number line, we need to find the average of these two fractions.

1. Convert the fractions to have a common denominator:
   \[
   \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2 \times 5}{3 \times 5} = \frac{10}{15}
   \]
   \[
   \frac{4}{5} = \frac{4 \times 3}{5 \times 3} = \frac{12}{15}
   \]

2. Calculate the average of these two fractions:
   \[
   \text{Average} = \frac{\frac{10}{15} + \frac{12}{15}}{2} = \frac{\frac{22}{15}}{2} = \frac{22}{30} = \frac{11}{15}
   \]

3. Convert the fraction \(\frac{11}{15}\) to a decimal:
   \[
   \frac{11}{15} \approx 0.7333
   \]

4. Identify the number closest to 0.7333 among the given options:
   - (A) \(\frac{11}{15} = 0.7333\)
   - (B) \(\frac{7}{8} \approx 0.875\)
   - (C) \(\frac{3}{4} = 0.75\)
   - (D) \(\frac{6}{15} = 0.4\)
   - (E) \(\frac{5}{8} \approx 0.625\)

The number closest to 0.7333 is \(\frac{11}{15}\).

### Answer: A) \(\frac{11}{15}\)
